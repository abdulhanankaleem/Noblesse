The House

Founded on the belief that tailoring is a form of restraint.

The House

We make clothes the way we'd want them made for us — slowly, and without apology for the cost of that.

Noblesse started as a two-person tailoring studio before it became anything resembling a brand. The name came later, and reluctantly — we'd rather the clothes carried the reputation than the word did. What hasn't changed is the standard: every pattern is drafted and graded in-house, every mill relationship is one we've held for years, and nothing leaves the atelier until someone who isn't us has worn it first.

Cut, not trend

Patterns are revised, not replaced. A jacket block we drafted in year two is still in use, refined but recognizable.

Cloth first

We choose fabric before season, and hold a design until the right cloth exists — sometimes for years.

Kept in stock

Core pieces don't get discontinued to make room for new ones. We'd rather carry fewer styles longer.

The Ateliers

Three ateliers, one standard

Our tailoring is finished in Biella, our knitwear in the Veneto, and our leather goods in a single workshop outside Florence that takes on no other clients. Each ships to a shared quality bench before anything is listed for sale.
